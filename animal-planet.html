A genus of snakes found almost exclusively in Africa. These snakes are often referred to as glossy snakes.

Some Species Found Within this Genus:

A. concolor

  • This species primarily in South Africa and Swaziland.
  • It is ventrally pale blackish purple.
  • It is typically about 10 inches long

Conservation Status:

Least Concern

A. dimidiata

  • This species is endemic to tanzania.
  • Is black dorsally and white ventrally.
  • Can reach up to 18 inches long

Conservation Status:

Least Concern

A. katagensis

  • This species is primarily in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Zambia.
  • It is ventrally pale blackish purple.
  • It is typically about 10 inches long

Conservation Status:

Least Concern

A. microphthalma

  • Primarily found in Mozambique and the Republic of South Africa.
  • It is ventrally white, and dorsally dark brown.
  • It is typically about 11.75 inches long

Conservation Status:

Least Concern

A. polylepis

  • Endemic throughout eastern and northern Sub-Saharan Africa
  • More rows of scales than any other of the genus
  • Typically about 15.75 inches long

Conservation Status:

Least Concern

A. unicolor

  • This species is endemic throughout Sub-Saharan Africa.
  • Often feeds on mice and geckos
  • May reach a total length of 14.25 inches

Conservation Status:

Least Concern
